Egg-and-spoon


Game details

An egg-and-spoon race is a sporting event in which participants must balance an egg or similarly shaped item upon a spoon and race with it to the finishing line. At many primary schools an egg-and-spoon race is staged as part of the annual Sports Day, alongside other events such as the sack race and the three-legged race.

In some schools, the use of raw eggs is banned on the grounds of health and safety and fears of allergy or of competitors contracting salmonella through accidental ingestion of the contents of a broken egg.[9] Punitive insurance premiums have also resulted in the cancellation of some events.The phrase "egg and spoon" features in The Concise New Partridge Dictionary of Slang and Unconventional English; its use, along with the idiom good egg with which it is sometimes confused, is frowned upon by the Metropolitan Police on the grounds of it being derogatory and rhyming slang for "coon".

Game rules

  • Competitors race either individually or in teams in the manner of a relay race.
  • If the egg falls from the spoon then competitors may be required to stop, retrieve, and reposition their egg.
  • fastest mile egg-and-spoon race holding the spoon with both hands (8 minutes, 5 seconds).
